# INVENTORY-RISK-ANALYSIS
Analyzing inventory data to uncover $122M in excess stock, identify supply chain risks, and support data-driven inventory decisions using Excel and Power Query.

This project analyzes inventory data from a growing distribution company that manages stock across multiple warehouses. The company sources products from various suppliers and aims to maintain optimal inventory levels while minimizing operational and financial risks.

# Case Problem
The company is facing challenges in managing its inventory efficiently. There are concerns that too much capital is tied up in stock, while some products may still be at risk of stockouts. Additionally, reliance on certain suppliers and inventory distribution across warehouses may be creating further exposure.

# Objectives
Identify overstocked and understocked products
Evaluate capital tied up in inventory
Analyze supplier dependency risk
Assess warehouse distribution and exposure
Provide actionable insights for better decision-making

# Tools & Techniques
Microsoft Excel
Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)
Pivot Tables & Dashboard Design

# Data Processing
The dataset was cleaned and transformed using Power Query. Key steps included:
Ensuring correct data types for numerical and date fields
Creating calculated columns such as Inventory Value, Excess Value, and Shortfall Value
Classifying stock into categories (Overstock, Understock, Critical, Adequate)

# Key Insights
Approximately $122M is tied up in overstock inventory, indicating capital inefficiency
A large proportion of products are overstocked, while some remain understocked or critical
Overstocking is consistent across product categories, suggesting systemic issues in procurement or forecasting
Supplier patterns indicate possible bulk purchasing behavior, contributing to excess stock
Inventory is evenly distributed across warehouses, but all locations carry high overstock levels

# Recommendations
Reduce excess inventory through controlled purchasing and stock optimization
Implement demand-driven reorder strategies
Review supplier agreements to avoid bulk-driven overstocking
Continuously monitor inventory health using dashboards
Focus on balancing stock levels to prevent both overstock and stockouts

# Conclusion
The analysis highlights that the primary challenge is not just excess inventory, but inefficient stock allocation. By improving inventory planning, supplier management, and monitoring processes, the company can reduce capital waste and enhance operational efficiency.
